Hi! I'm **Yanqing Shi (施延庆)**.

I am a PhD student at [The University of Hong Kong (HKU)](https://www.hku.hk/), Department of Computer Science. My research focuses on **3D perception and reconstruction for surgical scenes**, **multimodal learning**, and **embodied intelligence**. I am particularly interested in building systems that bridge 2D vision to 3D understanding in challenging medical environments — dealing with dynamic tissue, occlusion, and complex lighting conditions.

Previously, I obtained my M.Sc. in Computer Science from HKU (supervised by [Prof. Grantham Pang](https://www.eee.hku.hk/~gpang/)) and my B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Automation from [Soochow University](http://www.suda.edu.cn/) (supervised by Prof. Lu Bo), where I worked on surgical robotics and continuum robot path planning.

My research interests include:
- **3D Surgical Scene Analysis**: NeRF/3DGS-based reconstruction for endoscopic and laparoscopic surgery
- **Multimodal Medical AI**: Attention-based blood pressure estimation from PPG and ECG signals
- **AI Agent Systems**: LLM-powered agent frameworks and medical RAG chatbots
- **Spatial Omics**: Computational methods for spatial transcriptomics (3D cell atlas reconstruction)
